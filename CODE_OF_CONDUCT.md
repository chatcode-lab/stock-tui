# Community Code Of Conduct

## Our Commitment

The `stock-tui` community is committed to a respectful, inclusive, and
harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size,
disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity or expression,
experience level, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal
appearance, race, caste, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We act in ways that support a healthy technical community and put the safety
of people above winning an argument.

## Expected Behavior

Community members are expected to:

- Be respectful of different viewpoints, backgrounds, and experience levels.
- Give and receive technical feedback about the work, not the person's worth.
- State assumptions and sources, especially for financial-data claims.
- Accept correction, take responsibility for mistakes, and repair harm where
  possible.
- Respect privacy, provider agreements, and requests not to share personal or
  credentialed data.
- Keep discussions relevant to the project and its users.
- Help maintain an environment where people can participate without fear of
  harassment or retaliation.

## Unacceptable Behavior

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- Harassment, threats, stalking, intimidation, or sustained disruption.
- Sexualized language or imagery and unwelcome sexual attention or advances.
- Insults, demeaning comments, deliberate misgendering, slurs, or attacks based
  on identity or personal characteristics.
- Trolling, bad-faith argument, or repeated refusal to respect a stated
  boundary.
- Publishing another person's private information without explicit permission.
- Exposing credentials, private caches, account details, or vulnerability
  reports.
- Pressuring contributors to provide financial advice or disclose positions,
  identity, employer, or provider account status.
- Retaliation against someone who reports a concern or participates in its
  review.
- Conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional
  open-source setting.

Disagreement, direct technical critique, and rejection of a proposal are not
harassment when expressed respectfully and focused on the work.

## Scope

This code applies in repository issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions,
project chat, events, and other spaces managed by the project. It also applies
when someone officially represents the project, such as through a project
account, address, or appointed role.

## Reporting

Report unacceptable behavior privately to `support@chatcode.dev` with the
subject `stock-tui conduct report`. Include links, dates, context, and the
outcome you are seeking when safe to do so. Do not confront the subject or
collect additional evidence if that would put anyone at risk.

For a software vulnerability, follow [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md) instead. For an
immediate threat, contact the appropriate local emergency or platform safety
service; project maintainers cannot provide emergency response.

Maintainers will protect the reporter's identity and report contents as far as
reasonably possible, sharing information only with people needed to assess and
address the situation. Knowingly false reports made to harm another person are
themselves a violation. An unproven good-faith report is not.

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for interpreting and enforcing this code. They may
edit or remove content, close or lock discussions, reject contributions,
issue a private or public warning, require a behavior change, temporarily
restrict participation, or permanently ban a participant.

Responses will consider impact, context, history, safety, willingness to
repair harm, and conflicts of interest. A maintainer named in a report must not
lead its review. Enforcement details may remain private to protect affected
people; maintainers will communicate the outcome to the reporter when
appropriate.

No contributor, maintainer, sponsor, or organization representative is exempt
from this code.
